Ruby vs. JavaScript

I would say that the scope of Ruby is greater than the one of Javascript.

Although Javascript has a lot of applications, it seems that its main focus is the front end side of things (user interaction, HTML manipulation, cookie manipulation, AJAX, etc.).

Ruby, on the other hand, seems more solid, versatile and powerful as a general-purpose language. But more than that, Ruby seems to have a quality I have never heard of regarding any other programming language: everywhere I go, I find software engineers excited about the "Beauty" of writing Ruby code. Just this quality makes Ruby a very attractive adventure to explore. Now put the extreme possibilities of this language when used with Rails, and you have a "killer cocktail".
